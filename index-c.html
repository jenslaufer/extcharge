What you'd gain

01

Trials your users can't reset

ExtensionPay computes trial validity in the browser — its own docs say so — and a documented exploit turned a 14-day trial into 7,978 years by faking the install date. ExtCharge enforces trial and paid state on the server. Same API call on your side; a different answer to console tricks.

02

Data that stays in the EU

ExtCharge is built and operated in Germany, GDPR-first. When an EU user asks where their data lives, you have a clean answer — and no US transfer to document in your privacy policy.

03

A rate we're aiming to set below 5%

Same model you already know — a percentage per transaction, no monthly fee, no setup fee, your money in your own Stripe via Stripe Connect. We're aiming a little under the going 5%; the final rate isn't locked, and early-access developers get a say.

What you'd risk

Let's be straight: ExtensionPay is good software. It's open source on the client, it's trusted, and your integration works. Switching to an unproven service would normally be reckless.

That's exactly why ExtCharge is protocol-compatible. The exit is the same as the entrance. If we're slow, wrong, or not for you — change the URL back. Your worst case is the five minutes it took. No data hostage, no rewrite, no sunk weekend. We chose an architecture where we have to keep earning you, because losing you costs you nothing.
