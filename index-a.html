The part nobody warns you about

Your extension is done. Charging for it is where the real project starts.

Stripe alone wants a server, a database, code to sync paid status into the browser, receipt emails, and someone — you — to keep it all patched. The founder of one payment service put it plainly: taking payments in extensions is "weeks of tedious work." And after those weeks, you own a second product: your billing stack. It pages you, not your users.

ExtCharge is that second product, run for you.

What you get

01

The whole billing stack, handled

Subscriptions, one-time payments, free trials, plans, coupons, payment history, analytics. Your extension calls a small client library; ExtCharge does the rest. Nothing for you to host, patch, or babysit at 2am.

02

Your money lands in your Stripe

You connect your own Stripe account. Every payment goes straight there — ExtCharge never holds your funds. We take a small per-transaction fee off the top, and that's the whole relationship.

03

Your customers stay paid. Everywhere.

Magic-link login makes the email the identity: a customer reinstalls or switches machines and they're still paid — no password resets, no angry support tickets. Works on Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Opera, and Brave, in 135+ currencies. And trials are enforced on our server, so users can't reset them from the console.
