Why a payments company would sell you its own exit

Every hosted payment service asks for the same thing: put your entire revenue stream behind our API and trust us. For a solo developer that's the scariest dependency there is — if the service raises rates, breaks, or disappears, your income goes with it.

We can't promise you'll never want to leave. So we built it so leaving is cheap. That changes who carries the risk: not you.

The three ownership guarantees

01

Your money never touches us

You connect your own Stripe account via Stripe Connect. Every payment settles directly there, on Stripe's rails, under your name. ExtCharge takes its small per-transaction fee and holds nothing. No payout schedule of ours to wait on, no balance of yours on our books.

02

Your integration isn't ours

ExtCharge implements the same API as the open-source ExtPay.js client — a protocol we don't own and can't fence. Your payment code stays portable the day you write it. Entering is one URL; so is leaving; so is coming back.

03

Your decisions actually hold

You set prices, plans, trials, and coupons — and they're enforced on ExtCharge's servers, not in the user's browser. A trial you set to 14 days ends in 14 days; no console trick resets it. Those servers run in Germany, EU-hosted and GDPR-first, so the data answer is yours to give too. Magic-link login keeps your customers attached to their email, not to our platform — payment history and analytics included.

Pricing

A small percentage per transaction, only when you earn. No monthly fee, no setup fee, no contract.

We're aiming a bit under the going 5%, and the rate isn't final: early-access developers help set it. If we ever stop being worth our cut, the exit is one line. That's the deal — and if it sounds like your kind of deal, the line below is where it starts.
