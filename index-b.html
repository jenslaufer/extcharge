The disclosure

Here's the whole attack, in your user's hands:

  1. 1. Open DevTools on your extension.
  2. 2. Find the stored install date your trial counts from.
  3. 3. Set it to the future. Done — the "14-day trial" never ends.

No code injection, no cracked build. Just edited local storage. This isn't hypothetical: it's a published write-up — and most extension paywalls work exactly this way, because the trial check ships inside the extension. That's not a bug in any one product; it's a structural choice. But the structure means every trial decision happens on hardware your user controls.

The impact: you can't see it. A reset trial doesn't show up as a failed charge or a refund. It shows up as nothing — a user who looks free forever. Every one of them is a sale you already earned and never collected.
